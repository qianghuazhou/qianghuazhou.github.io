Diagnosis
Needle biopsy of subcarinal lymph node (consultation materials from Humber river Hospital): non-necrotizing granulomatous lymphadenitis, see comment
Comment
The histology is first of all reminiscent of sarcoidosis. Clinical correlation is needed for confirmation.
An infectious etiology is not excluded, but is less likely.
Microscopic Description
These biopsies show fibrous tissue with scattered small granulomas without necrosis. A scattering of small lymphoid cells is seen within the granulomas and within the fibrous tissue.
Granulomas are highlighted by CD31, CD68 staining and show, as expected, also a number of S100+ cells. The lymphoid cells are predominantly small CD3+ T cells within the granulomas, and both small CD3+ T cells and CD20+ B cells in the fibrous tissue. CD10 and BCL6 are not expressed. Histochemistry (PAS, GMS and AFB) do not reveal fungi or acid-fast bacilli.
Flow cytometry (see original report): B, T and NK cells with unremarkable immunophenotype



DIAGNOSIS

Subcarinal lymph node, needle biopsy:
– Non-necrotizing granulomatous lymphadenitis (see comment).

COMMENT

The histologic findings are most suggestive of sarcoidosis. Clinical correlation is recommended for confirmation.
An infectious etiology cannot be entirely excluded; however, special stains for microorganisms are negative and an infectious process is considered less likely.

MICROSCOPIC DESCRIPTION

Sections show fibrous tissue containing scattered small, well-formed granulomas without necrosis. A sparse population of small lymphoid cells is present both within the granulomas and within the surrounding fibrous tissue.

Immunohistochemical studies show that the granulomas are highlighted by CD68 and CD31 and contain scattered S100-positive dendritic cells. The associated lymphoid population consists predominantly of small CD3-positive T cells within the granulomas, with a mixture of CD3-positive T cells and CD20-positive B cells in the surrounding fibrous tissue. CD10 and BCL6 are negative.

Special stains (PAS, GMS, and AFB) do not demonstrate fungi or acid-fast bacilli.
