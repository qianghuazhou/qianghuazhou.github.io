The infiltrate features a of population of pleomorphic large lymphocytes with irregular ovoid nuclear contours, coarse chromatin with prominent nucleoli and pale eosinophilic cytoplasm. Occasional Hodgkin/Reed-Sternberg-like as well as bizarre/anaplastic forms are seen. The atypical cells are found in a polymorphic background of variably abundant small mature lymphocytes and histiocytes. Scattered mitoses and karyorrhectic debris are seen within the infiltrate. Focal angioinvasion/destruction is present. No follicular architecture or low-grade component is evident, nor is there any definite brain tissue sampled.

Immunohistochemical stains performed at your institution and/or BC Cancer on blocks A1 and A5 show that the atypical large lymphocytes are CD20+ CD19+ PAX5+ B cells which coexpress BCL6, MUM1, BCL2, CD21, CD30(variable) and EBER(ish), and show partial/weak expression of Cyclin D1. They are negative for CD10, c-MYC(<40%), CD5 and SOX11. CD3 and CD5 highlight background small T cells. An immunostain for CD21 does not uncover any follicular dendritic cell meshworks. The Ki67 proliferative index of the large cells is variable, but approximately 60% overall.

In summary, the findings are consistent with the diagnosis of an EBV-positive diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (EBV+ DLBCL). Clinical and laboratory correlation is recommended to exclude an underlying immune deficiency/dysregulation disorder. FISH studies for MYC, BCL2 and BCL6 gene rearrangements as well as LExA120 gene expression profiling are currently pending in our laboratory and will be reported separately.
