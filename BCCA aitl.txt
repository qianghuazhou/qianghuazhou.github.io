LYMPH NODE, RIGHT INGUINAL (CORE BIOPSY):
- SUSPICIOUS FOR NODAL T-FOLLICULAR HELPER CELL LYMPHOMA

H&E-stained sections show a core biopsy of lymph node with architectural effacement by an atypical polymorphic lymphoid infiltrate. The infiltrate is composed of a mixture of small lymphocytes, larger immunoblastic cells, abundant plasma cells, and scattered eosinophils, neutrophils and histiocytes. There is an atypical subset of small to medium-sized lymphocytes with round to mildly irregular nuclear contours, moderately condensed chromatin with small nucleoli and variable amounts of cytoplasm. Rare "haloed" forms are seen. Scattered mitoses are present within the infiltrate. Vascularity is moderately increased, but without significant arborization. A rare multinucleated giant cell is seen, but there is no well-formed granulomatous inflammation. Sheets of large lymphocytes, definitive Hodgkin/Reed-Sternberg cells or necrosis are not present.

Immunostains performed on block A1 show the infiltrate includes an atypical population of small to medium-sized CD3+ CD4+ T-cells which coexpress PD-1(strong) and ICOS, and show partial staining for BCL6 and CXCL13. These cells also express CD5, show aberrantly diminished expression of BCL2, and are negative for CD10. CD8 highlights fewer small T-cells within the infiltrate. CD20 and CD79 highlight B-cells in residual follicles as well as scattered larger immunoblastic forms. CD30 highlights these immunoblasts, but also shows variable staining of smaller cells, favored to reflect partial expression by the atypical T-cells (approx. 20%). The immunoblasts are negative for CD15. B-cells are negative for CD5 and Cyclin D1. Focal germinal centers are positive for CD10 and BCL6 but appropriately negative for BCL2. Kappa and lambda immunostains show a kappa light chain excess among the plasma cells and immunoblasts. EBER(ish) highlights few scattered variably-sized cells throughout the infiltrate, favored to be B-cells. CD21 and CD23 immunostains highlight follicular dendritic cell meshworks which are restricted to B-cell areas, but also surround entrapped vessels. Cytokeratin AE1/AE3 is negative for metastatic carcinoma. The Ki67 proliferative index is elevated within the infiltrate, averaging 60%.

Flow cytometry performed on representative tissue shows the lymphoid gate accounts for 88% of total events. B-cells account for 37% of the lymphoid gate. They show polytypic expression of surface immunoglobulin light chains and do not exhibit any significant immunophenotypic abnormalities. T-cells account for 51% of the lymphoid gate with the CD4-positive and CD8-positive cells in a 1.9:1 ratio. Accounting for approx. 40% of T-cells is an atypical CD2+, CD3+, CD5+, CD7+, CD4+, CD8(-/partial dim+), CD10-, CD56-, CD57-, CD200+, CD279+, CD45RA(-/partial+), CD45RO+ population of cells with a skewed TRBC1+ phenotype.

In summary, the findings are suspicious for a nodal T-follicular helper lymphoma (nTFHL), yet a definitive diagnosis is not possible in this limited core biopsy specimen. PCR T- and B-cell clonality testing will be attempted on the remainder of this biopsy and reported separately, but nevertheless an excisional biopsy with lymphoma protocol is required for definitive diagnosis and subclassification.
