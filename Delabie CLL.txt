Diagnosis
Needle biopsies of right inguinal lymph node (consultation materials from Humber River Hospital): small lymphocytic lymphoma / chronic lymphocytic leukemia with aggressive histologic features and extensive plasma cell differentiation
Comment
Thank you for sharing this unusual biopsy. Note is made of a history of chronic lymphocytic leukemia since 2009. The immunophenotype of the current lesion, is diagnostic of chronic lymphocytic leukemia. However, extensive plasma cell differentiation is seen (underscored by IGL staining) and the proliferation rate is high. The latter feature indicates more aggressive disease.
There is no evidence of large cell transformation.

Morphological description
This lymph node shows diffuse infiltration with small to intermediate lymphoid cells with round to oval nuclei with or without a prominent central nucleolus. Many cells show prominent Dutcher bodies or plasma cell differentiation. Some cells appear as small signet ring cells. Other cells have clear cytoplasm.
