EXTRANODAL ROSAI-DORFMAN DISEASE
All parts show similar histologic findings and are described together. H&E stained sections show portions of soft tissue containing an extensive proliferation of large histiocytes in a lymphoplasmacytic inflammatory background. The histiocytes feature round nuclei, pale chromatin with variably prominent nucleoli and abundant pale cytoplasm showing frequent emperipolesis by lymphocytes. Lymphocytes are small in size and show no significant atypia. Frequent lymphoid follicles containing reactive-appearing germinal center reactions are present. No brain tissue is identified in part 3. Sheets of large lymphocytes, diagnostic Hodgkin/Reed-Sternberg cells or evidence of metastatic carcinoma are absent.
 
Immunohistochemical stains performed at your institution and/or BC Cancer on blocks 2C, 2D, 2F and 3B show that histiocytes coexpress CD68, CD163(partial), S100(diffuse), Cyclin D1, BCL6 and BCL2, and are negative for CD1a, CD15, CD34 and CD117. CD20 and PAX5 highlight B-cells in follicles surrounded by CD3+ CD5+ T-cells in a reactive pattern. CD10 and BCL6 highlight germinal center reactions which are appropriately negative for BCL2. B-cells are negative for Cyclin D1. MUM1 highlights plasma cells, which show polytypic expression of kappa and lambda light chains. EBER(ish) is negative. An immunostain for cytokeratin AE1/AE3 is negative for metastatic carcinoma. The Ki67 proliferative index is very low within the histiocytes (less than 10%).

Limited panel flow cytometry (BF24-813) performed at BC Cancer on representative tissue from the left scalp shows no diagnostic immunophenotypic abnormalities.

In summary, the findings in all parts are consistent with involvement by extranodal Rosai-Dorfman disease. There is no evidence of metastatic carcinoma. An immunostain against BRAF V600E is pending and will be reported in an addendum. Correlation with clinical and imaging findings is recommended for full evaluation.
