SYNOPTIC REPORT
_

COMMENT
The morphologic and immunophenotypic findings are most in keeping with reactive lymphadenopathy which has a broad differential and includes autoimmune, infectious and neoplastic causes. The biopsies evaluated were relatively small but are negative for evidence of involvement by a lymphoproliferative disorder. Should the clinical concern for a neoplastic process continue after other causes are ruled out an excisional biopsy could be considered. Correlation with clinical, serologic, and other laboratory information is advised.

MICROSCOPIC DESCRIPTION
Sections from the core biopsies show a lymph node with expanded paracortical areas and varying sized follicles including germinal centers and tingible body macrophages. There is polarization of the cells within the germinal centers.  Scattered histiocytes are present throughout the node.

IMMUNOHISTOCHEMISTRY
The germinal centers have a reactive staining pattern; they are positive for CD20, CD10, and BCL6. They are negative for BCL2, CD43, CD5, and CD3.
CD21 highlights the dendritic cell meshworks. 
CD3, CD5, BCL2, and CD43 highlight T cells, predominately in the interfollicular areas.
MUM1 highlights only scattered cells.
The Ki67 proliferation index is high (approximately 80%) in the germinal centers and lower (approximately 5%) in the interfollicular cells.

COMMENT

The biopsies evaluated were relatively small but are NEGATIVE for evidence of involvement by a lymphoproliferative disorder. Sections from the core biopsies show a lymph node with expanded paracortical areas and varying sized follicles including germinal centers and focal areas with tingible body macrophages. There is dilation of the sinuses by unremarkable histiocytes.

CD21 highlights the intact dendritic cell meshworks. 
CD68 highlights the increased macrophages in the sinus space.

The Ki67 proliferation index is approximately 5%.
