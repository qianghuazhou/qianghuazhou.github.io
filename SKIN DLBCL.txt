DIAGNOSIS

Skin, right arm, biopsy:
– Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma, see comment.

COMMENT

The morphologic and immunophenotypic findings are consistent with diffuse large B-cell lymphoma. The proliferation index (Ki-67) is approximately 70%. Correlation with clinical staging studies is recommended to determine whether this represents primary cutaneous involvement or secondary involvement by systemic lymphoma.

MICROSCOPIC DESCRIPTION

Sections of skin show a diffuse lymphoid infiltrate within the dermis composed predominantly of large atypical lymphoid cells with pleomorphic nuclear features. Many cells contain one to several prominent nucleoli. The neoplastic cells are admixed with scattered small mature lymphocytes. Mitotic figures are frequent. Scattered macrophages containing apoptotic debris are present.

IMMUNOHISTOCHEMISTRY

Immunohistochemical stains demonstrate that the atypical lymphoid cells express CD20, BCL6, and BCL2. The cells are negative for CD3, CD4, CD8, and CD30. The Ki-67 proliferation index is approximately 70%.
