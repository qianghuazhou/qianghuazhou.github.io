Primary cutaneous CD8 positive aggressive epidoermotropic cytotoxic T-cell lymphoma
Thank you for sharing this interesting case with us.  Received for review from Kelowna General Hospital are 6 slides and 1 block designated case SK17-5480S corresponding to a left eyebrow skin biopsy performed on March 15, 2017.

Microscopic examination of the submitted material reveals a small punch biopsy of skin.  At the edge of the deep margin within the dermis is a focal atypical lymphoid infiltrate composed of large sized atypical lymphocytes with irregular nuclear contours, open chromatin, variably prominent nucleoli, and scant cytoplasm. 

Immunohistochemical stains performed by your laboratory show the atypical lymphocytes are CD45+, CD3+ T-cells that are negative for CD5 and CD43.  CD20 highlights rare small B-cells only.  Immunohistochemical stains performed at the BCCA show the atypical T-cells are positive for CD7 and negative for CD2.  Additional stains, including CD4 and CD8, were attempted but the infiltrate is not present in those sections and cannot be further characterized.  

Overall, the finding of large atypical T-cells that lack expression of CD2 and CD5 is most consistent with persistence/recurrence of the patient's previously diagnosed primary cutaneous CD8 positive aggressive epidermotropic cytotoxic T-cell lymphoma (SK16-23348/BC16-2451).
