A: BREAST, LEFT, 12:00 (CORE BIOPSIES):
- DIFFUSE LARGE B-CELL LYMPHOMA

Microscopic Description
H&E-stained sections show core biopsies of an atypical lymphoid infiltrate in a diffuse pattern. The infiltrate is composed of sheets of pleomorphic centroblastic large cells with oval nuclear contours, pale chromatin with prominent nucleoli and scant cytoplasm. Scattered mitoses and occasional karyorrhexis are seen within the infiltrate. An area of necrosis is also present. No follicular architecture or low-grade component is evident.

Immunohistochemical stains performed on block A1 show that the atypical large lymphocytes are CD20+ PAX5+ B-cells which coexpress CD10(weak), BCL6, MUM1, BCL2(diffuse) and c-MYC(80%). They are negative for CD5, Cyclin D1 and cytokeratins. CD30 highlights few scattered cells within the infiltrate (approx. 1%). CD3 and CD5 highlight background small T cells. CD21 demonstrates an absence of follicular dendritic cell meshworks within the infiltrate. The Ki67 proliferative index of the large cells approaches 100%.

In summary, the findings are consistent with a diagnosis of an aggressive B cell lymphoma with diffuse large B cell (DLBCL) morphology and germinal center B cell phenotype (Hans criteria) showing dual expression of MYC and BCL2. These features raise concern for a MYC/BCL2-rearranged "double-hit lymphoma", and therefore final classification of this lesion requires correlation with the results of FISH studies for MYC, BCL2 and BCL6 gene rearrangements. These studies as well as LExA120 gene expression profiling are currently pending in our laboratory; results will be reported separately. An EBER in situ hybridization stain is also pending and will be reported in an addendum.

The neoplastic B-cells are negative for EBER(ish) and positive for CD19 by immunohistochemistry.
