DIAGNOSIS
EXTENSIVE INVOLVEMENT BY A HIGH GRADE B-CELL LYMPHOPROLIFERATIVE DISORDER, with overall features compatible with BURKITT LYMPHOMA.

COMMENT
Correlation with clinical and other laboratory findings is suggested.

MICROSCOPIC
The soft tissue is completely effaced by a population of intermediate sized cells with round to irregular nuclei, condensed chromatin, indistinct to small nucleoli and small amounts of cytoplasm. Frequent tangible body macrophages are present giving a “starry sky” appearance. Mitotic figures are readily apparent.

IMMUNOHISTOCHEMISTRY
Immunohistochemical stains performed on paraffin sections show the atypical cells positive for CD20, MYC, BCL6 and CD10. They are negative for CD3, BCL2, CD34, and TdT.
The Ki-67proliferation index is >99% of the atypical cells. 
In-situ hybridization study for Epstein-Barr Virus encoded small nuclear RNA's (EBER) is negative.
