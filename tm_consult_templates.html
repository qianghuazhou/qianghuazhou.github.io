Acute Hemolytic Reaction

Reaction

TRANSFUSION MEDICINE CONSULTATION

Patient: [Name / MRN]
Date: [Date]
Requested by: Dr. [Name]

Reason for consultation:
Evaluation of suspected acute transfusion reaction.

Clinical information:
Patient developed [fever / hypotension / hemoglobinuria / back pain] during or shortly after transfusion of [RBC / platelet / plasma].

Investigations reviewed:
• Clerical check
• Direct antiglobulin test (DAT)
• Hemolysis laboratory studies (bilirubin, LDH, haptoglobin)
• Repeat ABO/Rh typing
• Antibody screen

Findings:
Clerical check: [no discrepancy / discrepancy identified]
DAT: [positive / negative]
Hemolysis markers: [present / absent]

Interpretation:
Findings are [consistent / not consistent] with an acute hemolytic transfusion reaction.

Recommendations:
• Discontinue implicated unit
• Provide antigen-compatible blood products if transfusion required
• Clinical monitoring for hemolysis and renal function

Communication:
Findings communicated to Dr. [Name].

Delayed Hemolytic Transfusion Reaction

Reaction

Post-transfusion hemoglobin decline with new alloantibody and hemolysis.

TRANSFUSION MEDICINE CONSULTATION

Requested by: Dr. [Name]

Reason for consultation:
Evaluation of post-transfusion hemoglobin decline.

Clinical information:
Patient recently received RBC transfusion and now demonstrates falling hemoglobin and biochemical evidence of hemolysis.

Investigations reviewed:
• DAT
• Antibody screen and identification
• Transfusion history

Findings:
New alloantibody identified: [e.g., anti-Jka].
DAT: positive.

Interpretation:
Findings are consistent with a delayed hemolytic transfusion reaction.

Recommendations:
Future transfusions should utilize antigen-negative RBC units.
Document antibody history in transfusion records.

Communication:
Results communicated to Dr. [Name].

Massive Hemorrhage Protocol

Urgent Support

Guidance for balanced component therapy in severe hemorrhage.

Requested by: Dr. [Name]

Reason for consultation:
Guidance regarding massive transfusion management.

Clinical information:
Patient undergoing massive transfusion for [trauma / obstetric hemorrhage / surgery].

Interpretation:
Balanced component therapy recommended.

Recommendations:
• RBC, plasma, and platelets in approximately balanced ratios
• Monitor fibrinogen level and consider cryoprecipitate if low
• Monitor calcium and coagulation parameters
