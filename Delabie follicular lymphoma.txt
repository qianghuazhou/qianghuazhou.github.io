Diagnosis
Needle biopsy of lymph node (consultation materials from Mackenzie Health, Richmond Hill Hospital): classic follicular lymphoma (grade 2, nodular and diffuse diffuse variant)
Comment
Follicular lymphoma, diffuse variant, is mostly diffuse, CD23+ and presents in the inguinal area, most often with a large mass. Irrespective of the latter, the stage is often low (I or II). BCL2 gene translocation is typically absent. The latter analysis, together with BCL6 gene translocation analysis has been requested and will be reported in an addendum.
There is no morphological (as well as immunophenotypic) evidence of splenic lymphoma as suggested by your flow cytometry data. Infiltration of lymph nodes by splenic lymphoma is very rare. It may be that some of the markers reported by flow cytometry (e.g. CD5, CD123) to be weakly expressed, may have been spurious. This may be suggested by our findings that repeated immunohistochemical stains for CD5 and CD123 could not demonstrate expression on the lymphoma cells.
