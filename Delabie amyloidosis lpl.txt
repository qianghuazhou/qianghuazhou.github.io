Diagnosis
Needle biopsy of omentum (consultation materials from Scarborough Health Network): massive amyloidosis with limited infiltration by lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma
Comment
Note is made of a previous diagnosis of lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma.
Microscopic Description
This biopsy shows massive amyloid deposition surrounded by a foreign-body type granulomatous infiltrate as well as by small aggregates of small lymphoid cells comprising lymphoplasmacytoid cells.
Immunohistochemistry: lymphoid cells comprise CD20+ cells and IGL+, IGK- lymphoplasmacytoid cells, as well as CD3+ T cells.
Histochemistry: amyloid is birefringent upon staining with congo red and polarizing light microscopy
Protein sequencing: IgM/IGL type amyloid


DIAGNOSIS

Omentum, needle biopsy:
– Extensive AL (lambda) amyloid deposition with focal involvement by lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma (see comment).

COMMENT

The patient has a prior diagnosis of lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma. The current biopsy shows extensive amyloid deposition with only limited aggregates of lymphoplasmacytoid cells, consistent with involvement by the known lymphoma.
Mass spectrometry–based proteomic analysis identifies IgM/lambda-type amyloid, consistent with AL (lambda) amyloidosis associated with the lymphoplasmacytic neoplasm.
Clinicopathologic correlation is recommended.

MICROSCOPIC DESCRIPTION

Sections show extensive extracellular amorphous eosinophilic material within omental tissue, forming large deposits associated with a foreign body–type granulomatous reaction.
Small aggregates of lymphoid cells with lymphoplasmacytoid morphology are present adjacent to the amyloid deposits.

IMMUNOHISTOCHEMISTRY

The lymphoid infiltrate shows:
CD20-positive B cells
Lambda light chain–restricted lymphoplasmacytoid cells (IGL positive, IGK negative)
Background CD3-positive T cells
These findings support involvement by lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma.

HISTOCHEMISTRY

The deposits show apple-green birefringence with Congo red staining under polarized light, confirming amyloid.

AMYLOID TYPING

Laser microdissection and mass spectrometry identify IgM/lambda-type amyloid, consistent with AL (lambda) amyloidosis.
